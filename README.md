## 3. Preceding Question Prompts:
 QUESTION : Why is climate change a global concern? Explain how greenhouse gases contribute to global warming.
#### CHATGPT: 
Climate change is a global concern because it affects the environment, human health, agriculture, water resources, and economies across all countries. Rising global temperatures can lead to extreme weather events, melting glaciers, rising sea levels, droughts, floods, and loss of biodiversity. Since the effects spread beyond national borders, international cooperation is necessary to address the problem.

Greenhouse gases contribute to global warming by trapping heat in the Earth’s atmosphere through the greenhouse effect. Gases such as carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O) absorb heat radiated from the Earth and prevent it from escaping into space. Human activities like burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes increase the concentration of these gases, causing the Earth’s average temperature to rise.

#### GEMINI:
 Climate change is considered a global concern because its effects—such as rising sea levels, extreme weather patterns, and disruptions to food security—transcend national borders. A change in one part of the climate system often triggers a chain reaction that impacts ecosystems and economies worldwide.

 The Greenhouse Effect and Global Warming

To understand global warming, it is essential to look at the **Greenhouse Effect**, a natural process that regulates the Earth's temperature.

1. **Solar Radiation:** The sun sends energy to Earth in the form of light and heat. Most of this radiation passes through the atmosphere to warm the planet's surface.
2. **Heat Emission:** The Earth absorbs this energy and then radiates it back toward space as infrared radiation (heat).
3. **Heat Trapping:** Greenhouse gases (GHGs) like carbon dioxide ($CO_2$), methane ($CH_4$), and nitrous oxide ($N_2O$) act like a thermal blanket. They allow sunlight to enter but prevent a significant portion of the heat from escaping the atmosphere.
4. **Temperature Rise:** While this effect is naturally necessary to keep Earth habitable, human activities (like burning fossil fuels and deforestation) have increased the concentration of these gases. This traps "extra" heat, leading to a steady rise in the Earth's average surface temperature, known as **global warming**.

Major Greenhouse Gases

* **Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$):** Released through burning coal, oil, and gas, as well as deforestation. It is the primary contributor to long-term warming.
* **Methane ($CH_4$):** Emitted during the production of fossil fuels, livestock farming, and the decay of organic waste in landfills. It is far more potent at trapping heat than $CO_2$ in the short term.
* **Nitrous Oxide ($N_2O$):** Primarily comes from agricultural activities, such as the use of synthetic fertilizers.
